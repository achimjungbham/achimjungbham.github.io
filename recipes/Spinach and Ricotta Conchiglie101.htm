Title: Spinach and Ricotta Conchiglie

Yield: 3 portions

Category: Main

Cuisine: Italian; vegetarian

Source: Vegetarian; p. 280

Ingredients

  • 20 large conchiglie (only need 15 but some will break)
  • 1 can passata
  • 400 g frozen chopped spinach
  • 3 slices white bread
  • 120 ml milk
  • olive oil
  • 250 g ricotta cheese
  • nutmeg
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 60 g Parmesan cheese; grated
  • 2 tbsp pine nuts
  • salt
  • pepper

Instructions

Pre-heat oven to 180 deg Celsius.

Thaw the spinach in the microwave oven, then put in a strainer to allow it to drain.

Cut the bread into small cubes and soak in the milk. It needs to be thoroughly dissolved.

Cook the pasta at very low heat for at least 15 minutes. Drain and rinse in cold water.

Squeeze the spinach to remove as much moisture as possible. Put in a mixing bowl with the ricotta and the bread. Add 3 tbsp of olive oil. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Mix well.

Mix together passata, garlic, another 3 tbsp of olive oil, salt and pepper. Pour into the white casserole dish (for 3x5 conchiglie).

Fill the conchiglie with the spinach ricotta mixture and place in the tomato sauce, leaving only small gaps between them.

Bake for 15 minutes in the hot oven. Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese and pine nuts and bake a little longer until the pine nuts begin to colour.
