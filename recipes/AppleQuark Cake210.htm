Title: Apple-Quark Cake

Yield: 1 Kuchen

Cooking time: 45 minutes

Preparation time: 2 hours

Category: Baking

Cuisine: German

Rating: 5.0/5 stars

Source: Die besten Backrezepte aus aller Welt

Ingredients

  • Dough:
    • 500 g flour
    • 30 g yeast
    • 60 g sugar
    • 1/4 ltr milk
    • 100 g butter
    • 1 egg yolk
    • 1/3 tsp salt
  • Quark filling:
    • 250 g quark
    • 50 g sugar
    • peel of one lemon, grated
    • 1 egg
    • 1/2 pack vanilla-flavoured custard powder
  • Fruit:
    • 600 g apples (Cox Orange)
    • 2 tbsp sugar
  • Glazing:
    • 1 egg yolk (optional)
    • 2 tbsp milk or light cream (optional)

Instructions

Prepare the yeast dough according to your favourite method.

Drain some liquid from the quark, then mix with sugar, egg, grated lemon peel, and custard powder.

Pre-heat oven to 210 deg Celsius.

Peel the apples and cut into 1cm sized cubes.

Butter a 26cm cake tin (Springform). Roll out 3/5 of the dough and line the tin with a rim about 3cm high. Pour in the apples and sprinkle with 2tbsp sugar (more if you are using Bramleys). Pour over the quark mixture. Roll out the remaining dough for a lid on top of the cake. Seal around the edge as best as you can but do cut a few holes for steam to escape. If desired, brush with a beaten egg yolk and milk mixture.

Bake in the hot oven for about 45 minutes. Once the top of the cake is nicely browned, you can reduce the heat down to 180, then 160 degrees.
