Title: Roasted parsnips

Yield: 4 servings

Cooking time: 1 hour 10 minutes

Preparation time: 15 minutes

Category: Main

Cuisine: Modern; vegetarian

Rating: 5.0/5 stars

Source: Ottolenghi Test Kitchen, p 96

Ingredients

  • Parsnips:
    • 1 kg parsnips; peeled and halved lengthwise
    • 10 cloves garlic; peeled
    • 2 cups olive oil
    • 1 tbsp flaked salt
  • Vinaigrette:
    • 2 red bird-eye chillies, finely chopped
    • 3 tbsp apple cider
    • 1 clove garlic; peeled and crushed
    • 1/4 tsp salt
    • black pepper
  • Garnish:
    • 1 tsp allspice
    • 1/2 bunch parsley; finely chopped
    • 1/2 bunch coriander; finely chopped
  • Pasta:
    • 400 g delicate egg noodles; eg fettucine

Instructions

Pre-heat oven to 160 deg fan.

Prepare parsnips and garlic. Place them in a roasting pan, pour over the olive oil, and sprinkle with the flaked salt. Cover tightly with foil and bake for 1 hour.

Meanwhile, prepare chillies and garlic, and mix them with the cider, salt, and a good grind of pepper. Chop the herbs. Relax.

Once the parsnips are ready, remove them from the oven. Heat a griddle pan to very high heat, add some of the parsnip oil, and char the parsnips in batches on both sides. This will only take a minute or so per side. Place them in a serving dish together with the roasted garlic.

Sprinkle with the allspice and the herbs. Use some of the parsnip oil to complete the vinaigrette and spoon this over the parsnips.

Cook your pasta according to instructions, drain them, and mix with the remaining parsnip oil.
