Title: Chicken with caramelised onion and cardamom rice

Yield: 4 portions

Category: Main

Cuisine: Modern Indian

Source: Ottolenghi Jerusalem

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 tbsp currants
  • olive oil
  • 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 kg chicken thighs with skin
  • 10 cardamom pods
  • 1/4 tsp whole cloves
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 300 g rice
  • 550 ml boiling water
  • 1 1/2 tbsp parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup dill, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cilantro leaves, chopped
  • 1/3 cup jogurt, mixed with 2 tbsp olive oil
  • salt
  • pepper

Instructions

Heat about 2 tbsp olive oil in a large sauté pan for which you have a lid. Fry the onion for about 10 minutes until deep golden brown. Transfer to a separate bowl.

Place the chicken pieces in a large mixing bowl and season with 1 1/2 tsp each salt and pepper. Add about 2 tbsp olive oil, cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon and mix with your hands.

Heat sauté pan with more olive oil and sear chicken pieces on each side for about 5 minutes each, then remove from pan also (the spices can stay). Add the rice, caramelised onion, 1 tsp salt, and plenty of black pepper. Add the currants as well. Stir well and return the chicken pieces, pushing them into the layer of rice.

Pour the boiling water over the rice, cover and cook for 30 minutes over low heat.

Finally, add the herbs and use a fork to stir them in and fluff up the rice. Serve with jogurt.
