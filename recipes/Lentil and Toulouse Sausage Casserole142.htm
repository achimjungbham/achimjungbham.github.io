Title: Lentil and Toulouse Sausage Casserole

Serves: 6

Category: Main

Cuisine: French

Source: The French Kitchen; p. 126

Ingredients

  • 300 g Puy lentils
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 onions, diced
  • 3 sticks of celery, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled and crushed
  • 3 sprigs thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3 large tomatoes, peeled and chopped (or one can of chopped tomatoes)
  • 2 tsp tomato purée
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 200 ml red wine
  • 400 ml water (approximately)
  • 750 g Toulouse sausages (or Cumberland)
  • large bunch of flat leaf parsley
  • 1-2 baguettes

Instructions

Soak lentils over night or pre-cook them in salt water for 10 minutes.

Heat the oven to 200°C.

Heat the olive oil in a casserole dish for which you have a lid that can go in the oven. Add the onion, celery, and garlic and sauté for 5 minutes.

Add the thyme (with stalks), bay leaf, tomatoes, and purée, mix together and cook for 5 minutes.

Add lentils, wine, and enough water to cover. Bring to a boil, then place the sausages on top, cover with the lid, and cook in the oven for 30 minutes.

Stir in the parsley just before serving.

Serve with with fresh French bread.
