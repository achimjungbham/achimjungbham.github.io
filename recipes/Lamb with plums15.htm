Title: Lamb with plums

Yield: 4 servings

Cooking time: 2 hours

Preparation time: 1/2 hours

Category: Main

Cuisine: Indian

Source: 50 great curries of India, p. 96

Ingredients

  • onions:
    • 4 tblsp oil
    • 2 medium onions; chopped
  • spices 1:
    • 4 cloves garlic
    • 1 piece ginger; walnut sized
    • 3 green chillies
    • 3 whole cloves
    • 4 green cardamoms
    • 2 1/2 cm cinnamon
  • spices 2:
    • 1/4 tsp turmeric
    • 1/2 tsp red chilli powder
    • 1 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • meat:
    • 700 g boneless stewing lamb
    • 1 tsp salt
  • fruit:
    • 1 1/2 cups plums
    • 250 ml lamb stock; or water
    • 5 sprigs coriander leaves
  • garnish:
    • 3 tblsp coriander leaves, chopped

Instructions

Chop your onions, peel the garlic cloves, peel and slice the ginger thinly. Wash, de-seed, and chop the chillies. Measure the spices. Cut the lamb into chunks. Chop coriander leaves. Wash and prepare the plums: half finely chopped and the other half cut into wedges.

In a deep skillet, heat the oil and fry the onions until they are golden and starting to brown at the edges. This will take 10-15 minutes.

Add the garlic (crushed), ginger (also crushed), cloves, cardamoms, cinnamon, and green chillies. Fry for 1 minute. Then add the turmeric, coriander and chilli powders. Stir well.

Take out all the spices and keep in a separate bowl. Add more oil to the pan and stir-fry the lamb pieces and the salt for 5 minutes over high heat.

Return the onion-spice mixture, cover, and cook the lamb in its own juices on medium heat for about 10 minutes. Stir again to cover with the spices.

Add the finely chopped plums, 5 sprigs of coriander leaves, and lamb stock (or water). Bring to a boil and simmer over low heat for 1 hour.

Finally add the plum wedges and cook for another 20 minutes. (Start your rice now.) Put in a serving dish and sprinkle with chopped coriander leaves.
