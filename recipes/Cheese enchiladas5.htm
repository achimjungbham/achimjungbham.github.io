Title: Cheese enchiladas

Yield: 4 servings

Cooking time: 10 minutes

Preparation time: 1/2 hours

Category: Complete meal

Cuisine: Mexican; vegetarian

Ingredients

  • Sauce:
    • 5 tblsp flour
    • 5 tblsp oil
    • 5 tblsp mild chilli powder
    • 1 clove garlic
    • salt
    • pepper
    • Worcester sauce (optional)
    • tomato paste (optional)
  • Salad:
    • 1 can black olives in brine
    • 1 iceberg salad
  • Enchiladas:
    • 1 large onion
    • 300 g medium cheddar
    • 8 corn enchiladas
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Beans:
    • 1-2 cans black beans
    • 1 tsp oregano
    • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup basmati rice (optional)

Instructions

Dissolve chilli powder in two cups of boiling water.
Put beans in pot (with juice) and heat. Season with 1/4 tsp salt and some oregano
Start rice (if using).
Shred cheese, chop onion, and mix together in a bowl.
Cut salad into fine strips. Slice olives into rings.
Preheat oven to 100 deg C. Put a serving dish into the oven.

Put oil and flour in sauce pan and brown slightly. Add chilli water a bit at a time, stirring vigorously.
Add crushed garlic. Season with salt and pepper. Add tomato paste and Worcester sauce to taste.
Keep on very low heat.

Heat a tblsp of oil to a pan. Heat up one enchilada at a time from both sides in the pan. Take out, put 1/8th of the onion-cheese mixture on top, and roll tight. Put into serving dish in oven to keep warm.

When all enchiladas are done, cover with the sauce and keep in oven for another 10 minutes.

Serve salad, olives, beans, rice, and enchiladas in separate dishes.
