Title: Sea Bass on Pita

Serves: 2

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 6 minutes

Category: Main

Cuisine: English modern

Source: Ottolenghi, p. 144

Ingredients

  • Salsa
    • 3 plump tomatoes, peeled and de-seeded, then chopped
    • 1 mild red chilli, de-seeded and cut into fine strips
    • 1/2 tsp dried chilli flakes
    • 1 tbsp chopped parsley
    • 2 tbsp chopped coriander
    • 3 tbsp lemon juice
    • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Fish
    • 4 sea bass filets (about 350g)
    • salt and pepper
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Bread
    • 4 (hand-sized) pita breads
    • 120 g thick Greek yoghurt
  • Garnish
    • skin of 1 preserved lemon, cut into very fine strips
    • seeds from 1/2 pomegranate

Instructions

Prepare and combine the ingredients for the salsa. Set aside.

Extract the pomegranate seeds. Finely slice the skin of the preserved lemon. Set aside.

Season the filets with generous amounts of salt and pepper. Heat some olive oil in a large non-stick (!) pan. Fry the filets for 3 minutes on each side.

Meanwhile warm the pita bread in a separate pan. Consider pre-heating two plates.

To combine, place two pitas on each plate and spread a thick layer of yoghurt on them. Put a filet on top and spoon salsa on top and around. Finish by sprinkling on some of the lemon skin and pomegranate seeds.

Serve immediately.
