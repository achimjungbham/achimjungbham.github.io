Title: Hanne Jensen Torte

Yield: 1 Cake

Preparation time: 1 hour

Cooking time: 1 hour

Cooling time: 1 + 1 + 2 hours minimum

Category: Cake

Cuisine: German

Rating: 5.0/5 stars

Source: Chefkoch.de

Ingredients

  • Sponge:
    • 125 g unsalted butter
    • 125 g sugar
    • 1 pinch salt
    • 1 tbsp vanilla-flavoured sugar
    • 4 egg yolks
    • 150 g flour
    • 2 tsp baking powder
    • 5 tbsp milk
  • Meringue:
    • 4 egg whites
    • 200 g sugar
  • 4 tbsp flaked almonds
  • Rhubarb filling:
    • 500 g rhubarb
    • 5 tbsp sugar
    • 40 g vanilla-flavoured custard powder
    • 1 pkg Tortenguss (or more custard powder)
  • Whipped cream filling:
    • 300 ml whipping cream
    • 2 tbsp vanilla-flavoured sugar
    • 2 leaves gelatine

Instructions

Preheat oven to 180℃ (or 160℃ fan)

Separate the eggs. Mix the flour with the baking powder.

Beat the butter with the sugar and a pinch of salt until creamy. Add the egg yolks and beat some more. Mix in the flour without much beating. Divide into two portions.

Beat the egg whites until stiff, add the sugar and beat until the sugar is disolved.

Use a "26cm spring form" (or two if you are lucky) and butter it well. Evenly spread one portion of the sponge mix and add a ring of meringue on the outside, about 1cm wide and 1cm high. Bake for about 12 minutes. Allow to cool completely, then transfer to a serving plate.

Clean the form and repeat with the other half of the sponge mix but bake for only 8 minutes. Allow it to cool somewhat but don't remove from the form. Cover it evenly with the meringue mixture and sprinkle some flaked almonds on top. Return to the oven for about 20 minutes. Now allow to cool completely in the form.

In the meantime, clean and peel the rhubarb and cut into small pieces. Cook with about 5 tbsp of sugar (more may be necessary if the rhubarb is very tart) until soft. Mix the custard powder and Tortenguss with a little milk and add to the rhubarb. Cook a little longer then allow to cool somewhat.

Beat the whipping cream and until soft peaks form. Soak the gelatine in a tablespoon of water and gently heat it until it dissolves. Don't allow it to boil! Add to the whipped cream and beat a little further until well mixed in.

If you have one, put a sleeve around the first sponge layer and spread the rhubarb mixture on top, then spread the whipped cream. Put in the fridge for at least 2 hours to allow to set completely. Only then place the second sponge on top. (If you do this too early, the weight of the second sponge will squeeze out the rhubarb and cream layers.)

Substitute the rhubarb with any other tart fruit such as gooseberries or tinned mandarines.
