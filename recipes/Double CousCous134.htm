Title: Double Cous-Cous

Serves: 4-5

Category: Main

Cuisine: English Modern

Source: Ottolenghi, p. 77

Ingredients

  • Oven-dried tomatoes:
    • 1 kg baby plum tomatoes
    • 2 tbsp muscovado sugar
    • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • salt and pepper
  • Giant cous-cous:
    • 250 g giant cous-cous
    • salt water
  • Regular cous-cous:
    • 250 g cous-cous
    • 300 ml light chicken or vegetable stock
    • a pinch of saffron
    • 80 g butter
  • Herbs and spices:
    • 1 tbsp tarragon leaves
    • 2 tsp nigella seeds
    • seasoning
  • Garnish:
    • 2 onions (optional)
    • olive oil (optional)
    • 1 tsp nigella seeds

Instructions

Preheat oven to 135℃ fan. Slice each tomato once lengthwise. Mix tomatoes with olive oil, sugar, salt, and pepper, then arrange on a baking sheet with the cut side up. Bake for about 90 minutes, releasing the steam that builds up in the oven about every 10 minutes.

Cook the giant cous-cous in boiling salt water like you would pasta. This takes about 8 minutes. Then drain and put in a large serving bowl.

Prepare stock and mix in the saffron strands. Pour over the (regular) cous-cous and mix well. Place the butter on top and cover to allow the cous-cous to absorb all liquid. Fluff it up with a fork then add to the giant cous-cous together with the tarragon and nigella seeds.

Add the dried tomatoes and their juices. Mix well but carefully.

If using, slice the onions very thinly and sauté for about 10 to 12 minutes in olive oil. Arrange on top of the bowl of cous-cous. In any case, decorate with another tsp of nigella seeds.

Serve at room temperature.
