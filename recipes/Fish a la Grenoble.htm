Title: Fish à la Grenoble

Yield: 2 servings

Cooking time: 6 minutes

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Category: Fish

Cuisine: French

Source: Wolfram Siebeck; p83

Ingredients

  • 300 g filet of white fish, eg cod
  • 60 g butter
  • 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp capers
  • salt

Instructions

Briefly rinse the fish under running cold water and pat dry. Peel the lemon and cut off the outer pith, then cut out the wedges so they are without any skin. Cut into small cubes. Drain the capers.

In a sauce pan gently melt the butter. Meanwhile slightly salt the fish on both sides. Sauté the filets for 3 minutes, turn over and sauté the other side likewise. Take out and place onto individual plates.

Add the capers to the pan and allow them to heat up. Distribute the lemon cubes on the fish filets, then spoon the capers with the butter on top. Serve immediately.

As Wolfram Siebeck says: Easy to prepare, easy to eat, difficult to beat!
