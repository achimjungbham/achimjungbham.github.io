Title: Spring Rice with Feta

Serves: 3

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 30 minutes

Category: Main

Cuisine: Modern English; vegetarian

Source: Meera Sodha, The Guardian

Ingredients

  • Rice
    • 1 cup Basmati rice
  • Sauce
    • 30 g pine nuts, toasted
    • 2 tbsp harissa
    • 1.5 tbsp tomato paste
    • 1 tbsp lemon juice
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 1/4 tsp salt
  • Pilaf
    • 1 large red onion, finely chopped
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 3 cloves garlic, peeled and crushed
    • 1 tin chickpeas, drained
    • zest of 1 lemon
    • 1 tsp allspice
    • 3/4 tsp salt
    • 1 small bag spinach, washed and chopped
    • 1 small bunch dill, leaves chopped
    • 200 g Feta, diced into small cubes

Instructions

Wash the rice and allow it to soak in fresh water while you prepare the other ingredients.

Toast the pine nuts for about 5 minutes over medium heat, stirring every minute or so. Mix with harissa, tomato paste, olive oil, lemon juice, and salt; set aside.

Chop the onion and sauté gently in the olive oil for about 8 minutes. Add crushed garlic towards the end. Add drained chickpeas, lemon zest, allspice, and salt, and mix well. Drain the rice and add it to the pan together with 2 cups of water. Bring to a boil, put on a tight-fitting lid, and cook on low heat for 12 minutes. Allow to rest for a further 5 minutes.

In the meantime, wash and chop the spinach, discarding any tough stems. Pick the dill leaves and chop as well. Cut the feta into small cubes.

Fold spinach, dill, feta, and pine nut sauce into the rice, cover with the lid, and allow to rest for yet 5 minutes more.

Now serve without delay.
