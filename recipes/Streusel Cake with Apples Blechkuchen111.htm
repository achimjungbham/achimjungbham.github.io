Title: Streusel Cake with Apples (Blechkuchen)

Yield: 1

Category: Cake

Cuisine: German

Ingredients

  • 400 g flour
  • 28 g fresh yeast
  • 35 g sugar
  • 185 ml milk
  • 40 g butter
  • 1 egg
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 kg apples
  • Streusel:
    • 350 g flour
    • 200 g sugar
    • 1 tbsp vanilla sugar
    • 200 g butter
    • 100 g currants

Instructions

Heat the milk carefully until lukewarm. Add yeast (dried one will do as well). Add a pinch of sugar and about a third of the flour. Mix well and leave to rise in the oven at 35 deg Celsius for 30 minutes.

Add the remaining sugar, the melted butter, the egg, and a pinch of salt, and mix well. Now add the remaining flour, mixing and then kneading all the time. Stop adding flour when the dough comes easily off the walls of the mixing bowl. Put it back in the oven to rise.

In the meantime peel the apples, quarter them and remove the cores. Cut once more into eighth or twelfth.

Grease a large baking sheet with butter and spread the dough. Place the apple wedges in a nice patters on top. Leave to rise again.

Pre-heat oven to 210 deg Celsius.

Now prepare the streusel: Mix flour, sugar, vanilla sugar, and melted butter, until nice crumbs form. Spread across the apples.

Wash the currants and spread those as well on the cake.

Bake for about 30 minutes.
