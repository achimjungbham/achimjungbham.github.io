Title: Hoi Sin Pork

Yield: 4 servings

Cooking time: 2 hours

Preparation time: 1 day

Category: Meat

Cuisine: Chinese

Source: Michal and Sylvia

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork; shoulder or leg
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp Chinese five-spice
  • 200 ml Hoi Sin Sauce

Instructions

1. Cut the pork into one or more big, longish and flattish piece (<= 2 cm thick).
2. Sprinkle salt and five spice powder on both size of the pork.
3. Smear the Hoi Sin Sauce all over the pork.
4. Marinade the pork for 2-3 hours (overnight is even better).
5. Preheat the oven to 170-180 degree Celcius.
6. Roast the pork in a non-stick oven dish (preferably with the lid on)
for 40 minutes. Turn the pork over and roast it for another 40 minutes.
Check the pork occasionally. If the pork looks too dry, put some water
(about 2 tblsp) in the dish (depending on the size of the dish).
7. Sprinkle some honey (<2 tblsp) on both side of the pork. Increase
the heat to 200C and roast for another 15-20 minutes. You may remove
the lid if you want to increase the BBQ effect. (Make sure that
the pork isn't too dry. If it becomes dry, put some more water into
the dish.)
