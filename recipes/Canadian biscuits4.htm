Title: Canadian biscuits

Yield: 20 biscuits

Cooking time: 12 minutes

Preparation time: 15 minutes

Category: Side

Cuisine: North American

Source: James (Joy of Cooking)

Ingredients

  • 2 cup flour
  • 2 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 80 g butter
  • 3/4 cup milk; minus one tblsp

Instructions

Preheat oven to 230C.

Mix dry ingredients. Add (cold!) butter, cut into pieces, work into breadcrumbs using fingers.

Add all at once the milk, mix with spatula or fork until most of the dry ingredients are moistened.

With lightly floured hands form into a ball and knead gently against the sides of the ball five to ten times, turning and pressing until the bowl is fairly clean. (Dough always seems too wet.)

Roll out to half an inch thick (1.5 cm). Cut out with a floured cutter (eg, drinking glass), or cut into squares with a knife.

Optionally brush top with milk.

Place on baking sheet at least an inch apart (if you want crusty sides) or otherwise closer together.

Bake until golden brown on top for 10 to 12 minutes.
