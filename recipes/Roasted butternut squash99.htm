Title: Roasted butternut squash

Yield: 4 portions

Category: Main

Cuisine: Middle Eastern; vegetarian

Source: Ottolenghi (Guardian)

Ingredients

  • 20 g butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large red onion
  • 1 large butternut squash
  • salt
  • 30 g pumpkin seeds
  • 1 tsp nigella seeds
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp ground turmeric
  • 4 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 green chilli
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 200 ml vegetable stock
  • 100 g Greek yoghurt
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander
  • 1 cup basmati rice

Instructions

Heat the oven to 200 deg Celsius.

Cut the onion into 1cm thick slices. Cut the squash into cubes of 3cm width. De-seed the chilli and cut into small strips.

Fry the onion slices in a large oven-proof saute pan for about 8 minutes. Add the butternut squash pieces and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it starts to colour.

Remove from the head and add half a teaspoon of salt, the seeds, spices, chilli, and sugar. Mix well and pour in the broth. Put in the hot oven and roast for 30 minutes.

Serve with yoghurt spooned on top, a sprinkling of chopped coriander, and a few nigella seeds.

Serve with Rice.
