Title: Upside-down rice

Yield: 4 portions

Category: Main

Cuisine: Middle Eastern; vegetarian

Rating: 5.0/5 stars

Source: Zaitoun, p. 142

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • Roasted vegetables:
    • 3 aubergines
    • 2 red peppers
    • 3 sprigs thyme
    • seasoning
    • 2 large tomatoes
    • olive oil
  • Broth:
    • 3/4 tsp cumin
    • 3/4 tsp coriander
    • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
    • 1/2 tsp allspice
    • 1 tsp turmeric
    • 1 pinch saffron
    • 1/2 litre stock
  • 1 clove garlic
  • olive oil
  • garnish:
    • 1 tsp mild chilli or sumac
    • fresh coriander

Instructions

Wash the basmati rice until the water runs clear.

Preheat oven to 180 deg fan.

Cut aubergines into 2cm thick slices, the peppers into thin strips, and the tomatoes into 1cm thick slices.

Spread a good amount of olive oil on a baking sheet and sprinkle with salt, pepper, and thyme leaves. Then place the aubergines and the peppers on it. Brush more olive oil on top of the aubergines slices and season again. Put in the oven for 25 minutes.

On a separate baking sheet arrange the tomato slices, again with a good amount of olive oil underneath and some seasoning on top. Add this to the oven about 10 minutes after you put in the aubergines and peppers.

Peal the garlic and slice very thinly. Prepare the (vegetable or chicken) stock and add all the spices. Add another 220ml of boiling water to thin the stock somewhat.

After the aubergines are roasted (they don't need to be fully done), take a large saucepan, add some olive oil (or butter) and roast the garlic slices briefly. Then arrange the aubergines neatly on top, followed by the peppers and the tomatoes. Spoon the rice over everything and pour over the hot stock. Bring to a boil on the hob, then add a sheet of kitchen foil tightly on top of everthing (ie, leaving no space between rice and foil), plus the lid. Return to oven for another 20 minutes but restrict to heat from below. Leave to rest for at least 10 minutes.

To serve, remove lid and foil and place a large serving platter on top of the saucepan. Now quickly invert pot and platter, and shake a bit so that it is evenly distributed inside. Remove the pan, garnish with sumac/chilli and chopped coriander leaves, and it is ready!
