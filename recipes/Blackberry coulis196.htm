Title: Blackberry coulis

Yield: 4 jam pots

Cooking time: 5 minutes

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Category: Preserves

Rating: 5.0/5 stars

Ingredients

  • 1 kg blackberries
  • 150 ml boiling water
  • 300 g sugar
  • 1 pkg vanilla sugar
  • 250 ml boiling water (optional)

Instructions

Wash the blackberries and drain well.

In batches of about 500g, thoroughly blend blackberries into a mush. Force the mush through a fine sieve to separate out the kernels and collect all juice in a jam pot. Save the kernels. This phase will yield about 500ml of juice.

Mix the kernels with about 150ml of boiling water and blend thoroughly again, then force through the sieve. This will allow you to extract about another 160ml of juice, plus of course the 150ml of water. All in all, you will have about 800ml of liquid.

Add sugar and vanilla sugar to the juice and bring to a boil. Let boil for no more than 5 minutes, check if you want to add more sugar (or some citric acid if you have overdone the sugar), and then pour into jam glasses. Close tight and let sit upside down for about 5 minutes or more.

You can now pour another 250ml of boiling water over the kernels and repeat the sieving process to get a refreshing blackberry flavoured drink (add sugar to taste).
