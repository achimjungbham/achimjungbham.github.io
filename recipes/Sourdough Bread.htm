Title: Sourdough Bread

Makes: 2 loaves

Preparation time: 5 + 5 + 5 + 20 + 20 minutes

Proofing time: 24 + 14 + 1 hour + 10 + 10 minutes

Baking time: 40 minutes

Category: Breads

Cuisine: German

Ingredients

  • Refreshing the starter
    • 60 g dark rye flour
    • 60 ml lukewarm water
  • Sourdough
    • 300 g white (or dark) rye flour
    • 300 ml lukewarm water
    • 4 tsp starter
  • Yeast pre-dough
    • 175 g strong white bread flour
    • 200 ml lukewarm water
    • 1.5 tsp dried yeast
  • Main dough
    • 18 g salt
    • 2 tsp coriander
    • 200 g white rye flour
    • approx. 150 g strong white bread flour
    • 250 g sunflower seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. Mix one tsp of left-over sourdough starter with 60 g of wholemeal rye flour and 60 ml of tepid water (discarding the rest). Keep at 26℃ for 24 hours.

  2. Mix four tsp of the freshened up starter with 300 g of white (or dark) rye flour and 300 ml of tepid water (discarding the rest). Keep at 26℃ for 14 hours.

  3. Mix 1.5 tsp of dried yeast with 175 g of strong white bread flour and 200 ml of tepid water. Mix well and cover with a moist cloth. Keep in the oven at 35℃ for 1 hour.

  4. Put about 5 tbsp of the ripe sourdough into a jar and keep in the fridge until the next baking session. Mix the remaining sourdough with the yeast dough, the salt, the coriander, 200 g of white rye flour, and the sunflower seeds (if using). Mix well with an electric mixer (or a strong spatula). Now work in strong white bread flour until you have a dough resembling a stiff paste (it will still be sticky). Leave to rest for 10 minutes, then knead for one minute, and leave to rest for another 10 minutes. Divide into two lumps suitable for your bread tins, knead a bit more, and snugly fit into the tins. Leave to proof for 60 to 90 minutes in the oven at 35℃ by which time it will have risen to (not quite) twice the height. Take out of the oven and cut the surface a few times with a sharp knife.

  5. Pre-heat the oven to 250℃. Set up the oven steamer (if it has that facility) or add a flat tray with boiling hot water at the bottom of the oven. Bake for 10 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 190℃ and bake for 30 minutes more.

  6. Immediately after taking the finished loaves out of the oven, baste their tops with water.

  7. Leave to cool and enjoy!
