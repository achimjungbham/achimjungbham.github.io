Title: Sourdough Bread

Makes: 2 loaves

Preparation time: 5 + 5 + 5 + 20 + 20 minutes

Proofing time: 24 + 14 + 1 hour + 10 + 10 minutes

Baking time: 40 minutes

Category: Breads

Cuisine: German

Ingredients

  • Refreshing the starter
    • 1 tsp sourdough starter
    • 60 ml lukewarm water
    • 60 g dark rye flour
  • Sourdough
    • 4 tsp sourdough starter from Step 1
    • 300 ml lukewarm water
    • 300 g white (or dark) rye flour
  • Yeast pre-dough
    • 175 g strong white bread flour
    • 200 ml lukewarm water
    • 1.5 tsp dried yeast
  • Main dough
    • 14 g salt
    • 2 tsp coriander (or some other spice mix)
    • 200 g white rye flour
    • approx. 100 g strong white bread flour
    • 250 g sunflower seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. Mix well one tsp of left-over sourdough starter into 60 ml of tepid water, so it is all dissolved. Add 60 g of wholemeal rye flour and combine. Keep at 24-26℃ for 24 hours.

  2. Mix four tsp of the freshened up starter into 300 ml of tepid water, so it is all dissolved. Add 300 g of white (or dark) rye flour and combine. Keep at 24-26℃ for 14 hours.

  3. Mix 1.5 tsp of dried yeast with 175 g of strong white bread flour and 200 ml of tepid water. Mix well and cover with a moist cloth. Keep in the oven at 35℃ for 1 hour.

  4. Put about 5 tbsp of the ripe sourdough into a jar and keep in the fridge until the next baking session (or use 1 tsp of it create another starter as in Step 1). Mix the remaining sourdough with the yeast dough, the salt, the coriander, 200 g of white rye flour, and the sunflower seeds (if using). Mix well with an electric mixer (or a strong spatula). Now work in strong white bread flour until you have a dough resembling a stiff paste (it will still be sticky). Leave to rest for 10 minutes, then knead for one minute, and leave to rest for another 10 minutes. Divide into two lumps suitable for your bread tins, knead a bit more, and snugly fit into the tins. Leave to proof for 60 to 90 minutes in the oven at 35℃ by which time it will have risen to (not quite) twice the height. Take out of the oven and cut the surface a few times with a sharp knife.

  5. Pre-heat the oven to 250℃ (220℃ fan). Set up the oven steamer (if it has that facility) or add a flat tray with boiling hot water at the bottom of the oven. Bake for 10 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 190℃ (170℃ fan) and bake for 30 minutes more.

  6. Immediately after taking the finished loaves out of the oven, baste their tops with water, then remove from the tins.

  7. Leave to cool and enjoy!

    Note: It's a good idea to refresh the sourdough starter every 4 days following Step 1.
