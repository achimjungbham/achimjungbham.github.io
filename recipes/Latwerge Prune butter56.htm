Title: Latwerge (Prune butter)

Yield: 2 4/5 kg

Cooking time: 1 day

Preparation time: 5 hours

Category: Preserves

Cuisine: German

Ingredients

  • 4 kg prunes
  • 400 g sugar
  • 1 stick cinnamon
  • 2 pieces star anise
  • 1 pinch ground clove (optional)
  • 1 pinch citric acid (optional)
  • more sugar (optional)

Instructions

Cut and remove stones from prunes. Put (cut side down) in a sufficiently large oven-proof pot with about 400g of the sugar. Leave overnight to draw some juice.

Next morning, heat on the hob WITHOUT stirring, to boil off moisture (initially setting 5 on our Miele induction hob until it bubbles nicely, then reduce to 4). This works fine for at least 5 hours and will get rid of about 30% of the moisture. If the juices become too thick, it's time to transfer to the oven as above. I recommend to use the hand blender to break it down completely before putting it into the oven. Add the cinnamon and star anis tied to a string, so it can be recovered easily.

Put in oven and set to about 140 degrees fan-assisted. Keep the oven door slightly ajar and check every 30 minutes or so and stir if the surface has become very dark. After about 3 hours the boiling process is finished (and will have removed a further 10% of moisture).

Take out of the over, add sugar to taste, a pinch of ground cloves, and perhaps some citric acid. Bring to a boil, stirring all the while, and fill into jam pots.
