Title: Swabian Red Currant Cake

Yield: 12 portions

Category: Cake

Cuisine: German

Source: Zwergenst√ºbchen Backbuch; p. 64

Ingredients

  • Pastry:
    • 300 g all-purpose flour
    • 100 g sugar
    • 175 g butter
    • 2 egg yolks
    • 1 lemon essence; or lemon zest
    • 1 rum essence; or one tbsp rum
  • Lining:
    • 1 egg yolk
    • 1 tbsp sugar
    • 3 tbsp plain bread crumbs
    • 50 g ground almonds
  • Filling:
    • 4 egg white
    • 200 g sugar
    • 500 g red currants
    • 150 g ground almonds

Instructions

Mix together (soft) butter, egg yolks, and sugar until smooth. Add flour and the flavourings. Add a some cold water (one tsp at a time) if necessary. Cover with cling film and leave to rest for about one hour in a cool place.
Roll out pastry and line a baking tin (26-28cm diameter) with it. Pastry will be quite sticky, so rolling out between two sheets of greaseproof baking paper may work better.
Set oven to 175 degrees Celsius.
Pierce the pastry many times with a fork. Stir the egg yolk with a fork and spread onto the bottom of the pastry shell. Sprinkle with a mixture of (plain) bread crumbs, ground almonds and sugar.
Beat the four egg whites until stiff, add the sugar and beat more until dissolved. Fold in ground almonds and red currants, and fill into the pastry shell.
Bake at 175 degrees for 30-40 minutes.
