Title: Mushroom rice pilaf

Yield: 6 portions

Cooking time: 2 hours

Preparation time: 25 minutes

Category: Main

Cuisine: Modern; vegetarian

Rating: 5.0/5 stars

Source: Ottolenghi Test Kitchen, p 127

Ingredients

  • Rehydrated porcini mushrooms:
    • 30 g dried porcini mushrooms
    • 500 ml chicken stock
    • 350 ml water
    • 1 1/4 tsp salt
    • pepper
  • Roasted mushrooms:
    • 500 g oyster mushrooms
    • 500 g portobello mushrooms
    • 1 large onion, halved and cut into 1/2cm thick slices
    • 2 red bird-eye chillies
    • 10 garlic cloves, peeled
    • 3 cinnamon sticks
    • 4 whole star anise
    • 150 g dried apricots, diced
    • 120 ml olive oil
    • 1 tsp salt
    • pepper
  • Rice:
    • 350 g basmati rice, washed and rinsed thoroughly
  • Garnish:
    • 3 spring onions, thinly sliced
    • 2 tbsp parsley, chopped
    • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions

Pre-heat oven to 220 deg Celsius fan.

Bring the mixture for rehydrating the dried porcini to a boil, then set aside.

Prepare all ingredients for the roasted mushrooms and mix them in a large bowl. Pour into a roasting tin and bake for 35 minutes, stirring after about 20 minutes.

Remove from oven and return half the mixture back into your mixing bowl. Mix the other half with the rice in the roasting tin. Bring porcini back to a boil and pour over rice etc without stirring. Cover tightly with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove from oven and leave to sit for another 10 minutes. Remove foil and mix everything if necessary.

Mix the reserved half of the baked mushroom mixture with the spring onions and parsley, add some more olive oil. Spoon this over the rice and serve.
