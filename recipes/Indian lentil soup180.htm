Title: Indian lentil soup

Yield: 6 servings

Cooking time: 1/2 hours

Preparation time: 15 minutes

Category: Starter

Cuisine: Indian; vegetarian

Rating: 5.0/5 stars

Source: The Times Magazine

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 2 tbsp ghee or sunflower oil
  • 1 onion; chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 50 g ginger; finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ghee or sunflower oil
  • 500 g carrots; coarsely chopped
  • 1 3/10 litre water or light broth
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • black pepper
  • salt
  • 150 g red split lentils
  • Greek yogurt
  • sesame oil
  • sumac

Instructions

Toast the fennel seeds in a large dry saucepan over medium heat for about 2 minutes. Then add ghee/oil and the chopped onions. Saut√© until the onion begins to soften. Add garlic and ginger and cook for a little while longer. Remove from pan and set aside.

Add second portion of ghee/oil and stir-fry the carrots until they begin to be caramelised. Add water (or light broth), turmeric, and generous amounts of salt and pepper. Return the onions to the pot and bring to a boil. If using a pressure pot, close it now and cook for 6 minutes. Otherwise, simmer for 45 minutes.

Blitz the soup base with a hand blender, check the seasoning and now add the red lentils. If you are using a pressure cooker then bring to a boil in the open pot, stirring all the time, and only then put on the lid. Using a moderate setting on the hob, cook for 10 minutes (the danger being that it will burn at the bottom of the pot). If you are using a regular pot, cook for about 30-40 minutes.

To serve, put two ladles full of soup in each plate, add a dollop of yogurt, drizzle some sesame oil over the top and finally sprinkle some sumac over everything.

A very eye-appealing dish! Serves 6 as a starter and 3 as a main dish.
